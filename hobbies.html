My Hobbies

I like to stay busy, whether that's competing, riding, or gaming, there's always something keeping my reflexes sharp and my mind engaged. Here's a deeper look at what I'm into.

Ping-Pong

More Than Just a Basement Game

Table tennis as most people call it is one of the fastest reflex sports in the world. A well-struck ball can travel at over 100 kilometers per hour, giving your opponent fractions of a second to react.

What I love most about the game is its combination of physical speed and mental chess. Every rally is a conversation: spin, placement, pace, and reading your opponent's body language all at once.

Techniques I'm Working On

Backhand Loop
A topspin attack from the backhand side. Requires precise contact point and explosive hip rotation.
Third-Ball Attack
Winning the point on your third shot after the serve. It's a cornerstone of aggressive, offensive play.
Short Push
A controlled, low return that stays short on the table, forcing a weak reply from the opponent.

Fun fact about the sport:

Table tennis became an Olympic sport at the 1988 Seoul Olympics and has been dominated by Chinese players ever since though the rest of the world is catching up fast.

Sport Motorcycles

Riding my Honda CBR600RR is one of the most focused experiences I know. You can't zone out on a supersport bike it demands your full attention every single second. In that way, it's a lot like ping-pong.

The CBR600RR sits in the supersport class, meaning it is built primarily for performance rather than comfort or touring. It rewards a skilled rider and punishes sloppy technique.

What I've Learned from Riding

  • Smooth inputs beat aggressive ones every time
  • Looking where you want to go is half the work
  • MSF training is worth every minute
  • Proper gear is non-negotiable ATGATT (All The Gear, All The Time)

Video Games

Gaming as a Serious Hobby

I've been gaming since I was a kid and it's never stopped being a major part of my life. I enjoy both competitive multiplayer titles and deeply immersive single-player story games.

What draws me to gaming is the same thing that draws me to ping-pong: the feedback loop of getting better. Every loss is information. Every win validates the grind.

Genres I Play

  • Fighting games execution and reads
  • First-person shooters aim and game sense
  • RPGs story and world-building
  • Racing sims because of course I enjoy those!
My Gaming Setup
  • High refresh-rate monitor for competitive play
  • Mechanical keyboard with tactile switches
  • Lightweight gaming mouse for fast tracking
  • Noise-canceling headset for immersion

Gaming and riding actually share more than people think. Reaction time, spatial awareness, and composure under pressure are skills that transfer across both.
